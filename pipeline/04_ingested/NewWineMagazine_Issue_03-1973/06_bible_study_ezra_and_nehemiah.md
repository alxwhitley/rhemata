# BIBLE STUDY: Ezra and Nehemiah
*by Howard Crafley*

## BIBLE STUDY: Ezra and Nehemiah
By Howard Crafley

## THE HISTORIC BOOKS

The name of the book of Ezra is from the author, Ezra, the priest and leader of Jewish exiles who returned to Jerusalem from Babylon in 457 B.C. He was a reformer, a transcriber of the Jerusalem Synagogue, and writer of I and II Chronicles, this book that bears his name, and most likely a part of Nehemiah. He also is attributed with revising and compiling the books that actually form the canon of the Old Testament.

The book of Nehemiah, presented here in a joint study together with Ezra, has been called by some the II Book of Ezra. It bears the name of Nehemiah, born in Babylon, the son of Hacaliah, who, when he was the cup bearer to Artaxerxes, was appointed by that king as governor of Israel.

## THEME – MAN RISES AND RE-BUILDS

Jeremiah had prophesied that the Babylonian exile would last seventy years (Jeremiah 25:11-12). At the end of the 70 year period the prophecies of Isaiah were shown to Cyrus, king of Persia, in which he was named by God as the benefactor of Israel, the restorer of Jerusalem and the temple (Isaiah 44:28; 45:1-4), which was made 140 years before their destruction by Nebuchadnezzar, or 210 years before Cyrus.

> Jeremiah 25:11-12
> Jeremiah 25:7
> Daniel 5:28
> Isaiah 44:22

Cyrus had come into power at the fall of the Babylonian Empire in fulfillment of prophecies. Responding to the response of Cyrus, the above mentioned prophecy concerning himself, Josephus wrote, "Accordingly, when Cyrus read this, and admired the Divine power, an earnest desire of the downfall of Haman and the pres- what was written; so he called for the most eminent Jews, that were in Babylon, and said to them, that he gave them leave to go back to their own country, and to rebuild their city Jerusalem and the temple of God."

The story of the return from exile and the rebuilding of the temple and the walls of Jerusalem is given in Ezra and Nehemiah. It centers around three personalities, Zerubbabel, governor of the Jews, Ezra, the priest and scribe, and Nehemiah, the governor, as leaders.

## OUTLINE

1. Zerubbabel:
(1) Decree and return. Ezra 1–2.
(2) Reconstruction and Opposition. Ezra 3–5. 
(3) Dedication of the Temple. Ezra 6.

II. Ezra
(1) Commission and return of Ezra. Ezra 7–8.
(2) Sin of the people confessed. Ezra 9.
(3) Sin of the people put away. Ezra 10.

III. Nehemiah:
(1) Reconstruction. Nehemiah 1–6.
(2) Restoration. Nehemiah 7–13:3.
(3) Reform. Nehemiah 13:4–31.

> *I am doing a great work, so that I cannot come down; why should the work cease, whilst I leave it, and come down to you?* (Nehemiah 6:3).

There are many types and lessons in Ezra and Nehemiah, together with the books of prophecy of the period, for both the daily Christian walk, as well as an authoritative message for the Body of Christ as a whole for this last day time. God again is calling a people out of Babylon (organized carnal confusion, based on self effort) to reconstruct His temple of "lively stones" (1 Peter 2:5) and restore the walls (Isaiah 58:12).
